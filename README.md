📊 Stock Market Explained for Engineering Students
🧠 What is Stock Market?
The stock market is a place where people buy and sell shares of companies
A share means ownership in a company
When you buy shares, you become a part-owner of that company

👉 Example: If you buy shares of Tata Consultancy Services, you own a small part of it.

🏦 How Stock Market Works
Companies need money to grow → they sell shares to public (IPO)
Investors buy these shares
Share prices go up and down based on demand and company performance
📈 Key Terms in Stock Market
1. Share / Stock
Small unit of ownership in a company
2. Stock Exchange
Place where trading happens
Example: National Stock Exchange of India (NSE)
3. Demat Account
Digital account to hold shares
4. Trading
Buying and selling of stocks
5. IPO (Initial Public Offering)
First time a company sells shares to the public
📊 Types of Stock Market
1. Primary Market
Companies sell shares directly to public (IPO)
2. Secondary Market
Investors trade shares among themselves
💰 Why Stock Market is Important?
Helps companies raise money
Gives investment opportunities to people
Builds long-term wealth
Supports economic growth
📉 Risk in Stock Market
Prices are not stable
Can go up or down anytime
Risk of loss if not studied properly
📊 Factors Affecting Stock Prices
Company performance
News and events
Market demand and supply
Government policies
Global economy
👨‍💻 Why Engineering Students Should Learn Stock Market?
Improves financial knowledge
Helps in smart investment
Useful for future income planning
Builds analytical thinking
📱 Real-Life Example

If you invest in Infosys:

If company grows → your money increases
If company performs poorly → value may decrease
📌 Advantages of Stock Market
High return potential
Easy online access
Long-term wealth creation
Passive income source
⚠️ Disadvantages
High risk
Market uncertainty
Emotional decision-making can cause loss
🎯 Conclusion
Stock market is a powerful financial system
It allows people to invest and grow money
Proper knowledge is very important before investing
Engineering students can benefit by learning early
